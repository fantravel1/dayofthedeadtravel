Why We Exist

Because cultural preservation requires intentional travelers.

Day of the Dead tourism is growing rapidly. With that growth comes a responsibility.

We saw too many travel sites treating Dia de los Muertos as a “bucket list experience” — ranking the “best party spots” and selling costume packages. The sacred was being flattened into the shareable. The deeply personal was being packaged for consumption.

DayOfTheDeadTravel.com was created as an antidote: a resource that puts understanding before itineraries, respect before Instagram, and families before tourists.

We believe travel can be a force for cultural preservation — when done with intention and care. When travelers arrive with context, with humility, and with genuine desire to understand rather than simply observe, they become participants in keeping these traditions alive rather than agents of their erosion.

"La muerte no es el final. Es un puente de flores entre los que aman."
— Death is not the end. It is a bridge of flowers between those who love.

Our Values

The principles that guide everything we create.

✿ Memory-First, Not Spectacle

We never frame Day of the Dead as entertainment. It is remembrance. Every guide we write starts from this truth. The celebrations you witness are not performances — they are acts of love between the living and the dead.

🌎 Regional Respect

Mexico is not one monolith. We honor the distinct traditions of each region — Oaxaca, Michoacan, CDMX, Puebla, Yucatan — without flattening their differences. What is true in Patzcuaro may not be true in Mixquic. We respect that complexity.

☠ Indigenous Voices

The traditions of Day of the Dead belong to the communities that created them — Aztec, Maya, Purepecha, Zapotec. We center these voices, not tourist perspectives. Their knowledge is the foundation of everything we share.

⚐ Honest Guidance

We tell you when a place is too crowded, when a tradition should not be photographed, when your presence might be unwelcome. Honesty serves you better than marketing. We would rather lose a reader than mislead one.

🌱 Ethical Tourism

We recommend locally-owned businesses, family guides, artisan markets. Your money should stay in the communities whose traditions you are witnessing. We do not take commissions or promote corporate tourism operators.

🕯 Grief as Sacred

Day of the Dead holds grief and joy together. We do not sanitize this. We do not make death “fun.” We honor the full emotional complexity of remembrance — the tears alongside the marigolds, the silence alongside the music.

✿ ☠ ✿

What We Are Not

Clarity about our purpose and boundaries.

☠ Let Us Be Direct

We are not a tour operator. We do not sell packages or take commissions.

We are not a party guide. We will never rank “best Day of the Dead parties.”

We are not a costume shop. We will never sell sugar skull makeup kits.

We are a resource for travelers who want to understand before they arrive. We provide context, cultural guidance, and honest information so that your presence at Day of the Dead is respectful, informed, and meaningful — for you and for the communities you visit.

☠ ✿ ☠

Our Commitment

What we promise to every reader and every community we write about.

  • Every piece of content is researched with cultural sensitivity. We do not publish first drafts. We do not guess. We take the time to get it right.
  • We consult with Mexican cultural practitioners and community members. Our guidance is informed by the people who live these traditions, not by outsiders looking in.
  • We update our guides based on community feedback. If a community tells us something is wrong or harmful, we listen and we change. Our content is living, not static.
  • We prioritize accuracy over engagement metrics. We will never sensationalize, exaggerate, or misrepresent a tradition to drive clicks. The truth is more important than traffic.
  • Free, accessible, and ad-free. This resource is available to everyone at no cost. We do not run advertisements. We do not sell your data. Cultural understanding should not be paywalled.
